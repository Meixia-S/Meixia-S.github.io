Life synompis

I was born in Hunan China and adopted at the age of three and a half. Due to speaking some chinese as a child my parents where keen on making sure I did not loose that skill. At 21 years old now my listening comprehension is near fulent with my other three skills leaving a bit to be desired as I have had little practice since high school. Growing up I was fortunate to be exposed to many different activies from art classes to chinese dancing to tennis lessons. These experince taught me how to learn different types of skills and knowing how I learn best as an individual.

At age 12 my mother passed away from heart cancer. This had a huge impact on my life as she was my main care taker. Soon after her passing my father started a six year traveling mania. Every break I had during middle school and high school, he would take me on eye opening vacations where we travled the world. Seeing what other people live through and what their normal life routine are made me truly value the life I had. During this time I was drawn to Japanese animation to the point it led me to taking Japanese language classes at the local community college COD. I made decent progress during that one semester and can say that learning languages is a low-key hobby of mine but it is hard to self teach a language with no one to practice with.

After graduating high school with honors I moved to Boston to attend Simmons University. I originally chose this school as I thought an all women learning envirnment would be the best way to start my higher education career since computer science is a male dominated field. After the first year this turned out not to be the case so I took the down time during COVID-19 to sit down and ask myself what I wanted my future to look like. I realized that sitting down and coding all day was not a long term goal. The idea of a dual major popped in my head. With that in mind I searched for a school in the area that offered that type of schooling. That is when I stumbled Northeastern University. It was a perfect match for both location and academic excellence so I applied immediately and was elated to be accepted as a transfer student. I am now in the third year and have really enjoyed my time at the university. .....

Passions

As I have entered the young adult stage of my life there where two distinct topics that really drew my attention. The first being web development/design and the second one being evolution. I will go into more detail as to what excatly makes those two very different interest become my passion.

I hope the layout and small details of this website (when finished) can in part show that web development is something I am truly passionate about.

Both my grandmother and mother have been very artisitic and distilled it's value in me when I was younger. At that time the more traditional way of creating art never appealed to me. It was almost a bit too open ended for me and if I made mistake, it often meant I would have to start over as arcylic paint is not the most forgiving medium. Web design on the other hand is quite the oppsite. One can simply remove a line of code or go back to an older version of the site to undo any mistakes. It is a more forgiving process in that aspect. It also mixes coding into the mix that more modern art form. Being in my early 20s this is an undienable plus since it makes me feel like my work can be appercaited by my peers and has real utility to it.

The second passion of mine is less techinally driven interest. Taking ecology and biology classes in high school made me look at the natrual world with a more critical eye. Instead of thinking an animal looked odd or bazzar, I started to wonder why they looked the way they did and if it was the environment or its place in the food chain. As I got older this curisoity turned to why humans are the way we are and our evolutionary journey and its place in the current time period. As more information closly gets uncovered and piece of our past has been put together it really presents an intriguing story of where we came from as a speicies. Learning about this kind of science has really helped me put things into perspective and feel more connected with the world. I know these are quite __ statements but I think someone's passion/hobby should help people feel more present and connected to their spot in the world and for me, learning about "life's" past does that for me. On a lighter note if you ever learn more about bugs, you'll find that they truly are the aliens that roam along side us!
